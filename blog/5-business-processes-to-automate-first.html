5 Business Processes You Should Automate First (And Why)

October 2024 8 min read

If you're running an SMB in South Africa, here's a sobering reality: your team likely wastes 20+ hours every week on repetitive tasks that could be automated. That's not a criticism — it's just what happens when you're focused on growing your business and haven't had time to step back and ask, "Is there a better way to do this?"

The challenge isn't whether you should automate. It's knowing where to start. Most business owners and IT managers look at automation and feel overwhelmed by the options. Which processes deliver the biggest impact? Which are quick to implement? How do you calculate the return on investment?

The good news: you don't need to automate everything at once. By focusing on five high-impact, low-complexity business processes to automate, you can reclaim 20-30 hours per week for your team. That's the equivalent of half a full-time employee — time that can be redirected toward strategic work that actually grows your business.

In this guide, you'll discover exactly which processes to tackle first, what the automated workflow looks like, and how to calculate your ROI. Let's start with the process that delivers the fastest payback for most SMBs.

Process 1: Invoice Processing

The Current Pain Point

If you're manually handling invoice processing, you know the routine: an invoice arrives via email, someone downloads the PDF, opens your accounting system, and manually types in the vendor name, invoice number, line items, amounts, and due date. Then you set a reminder to follow up on payment. When payment arrives, you manually match it to the invoice and mark it paid.

For most SMBs processing 20-40 invoices per week, this consumes 5-8 hours of someone's time. And that's assuming no errors — which is rarely the case when humans are copying data between systems.

The Automated Workflow

Here's what invoice processing looks like when you implement workflow automation:

  1. Invoice received via email attachment or file upload to a designated folder
  2. OCR (Optical Character Recognition) extracts data automatically — vendor details, invoice number, line items, amounts, due date, even tax information
  3. Data syncs to your accounting system (QuickBooks, Xero, or whatever you use) with no manual entry
  4. Payment reminder emails sent automatically — three days before due date, on due date, and seven days after if still unpaid
  5. Payment received and auto-reconciled against the invoice when your bank feed shows the transaction

The ROI

Time Saved: 5-8 hours per week

Error Reduction: 95% (eliminating manual data entry eliminates most errors)

Tools Required: Make.com (or Zapier) + your accounting software + Gmail

Return on Investment: If you process 20 invoices per week at 15 minutes each, that's 5 hours. At a conservative R500 per hour, you're looking at R130,000 per year in recovered time. Most invoice automation implementations pay for themselves within 6-8 weeks.

The real kicker? Your accounts payable accuracy improves dramatically, late payment fees drop, and you can take advantage of early payment discounts you were previously missing.

Process 2: Customer Onboarding

The Current Pain Point

Every new customer represents a flurry of manual tasks: sending welcome emails, setting up user accounts, collecting necessary documents (ID copies, signed contracts, compliance paperwork), scheduling the first consultation, and notifying the account manager. For service-based businesses, this easily consumes 3-5 hours per new customer.

The bigger problem isn't just time — it's consistency. When onboarding is manual, some customers get a stellar experience while others fall through the cracks. Your first impression becomes a matter of who happened to be in the office that day.

The Automated Workflow

A properly automated customer onboarding sequence looks like this:

  1. New customer signup triggers the workflow — this could be a form submission on your website, a deal marked "won" in your CRM, or a signed contract uploaded
  2. Welcome email sequence launches automatically — five carefully crafted emails over two weeks that introduce your team, explain next steps, and set expectations
  3. User accounts created automatically in your systems with appropriate access levels
  4. Document collection form sent via email with clear instructions on what's needed (ID, proof of address, signed agreements)
  5. First consultation auto-scheduled using Calendly or similar tool integration
  6. Account manager notified with a summary of the new customer and any special requirements

The ROI

Time Saved: 3-5 hours per new customer

Benefit Beyond Time: Consistent customer experience, faster time-to-value, zero missed steps

Real-World Example: A Cape Town legal firm reduced their client onboarding time from two days to two hours. More importantly, every client now receives the same professional experience, regardless of which partner brought them in.

For businesses onboarding 4-8 new customers per month, this automation recovers 15-40 hours monthly. That's time your team can spend on actual delivery instead of administrative setup.

Process 3: Report Generation

The Current Pain Point

Monday morning arrives, and someone on your team starts the weekly ritual: logging into your CRM to pull sales numbers, opening the accounting system for financial data, checking your marketing platform for campaign performance, copying everything into a spreadsheet, formatting it to look presentable, generating a PDF, and emailing it to stakeholders.

This process — repeated weekly or monthly for various reports — consumes 4-6 hours per week for most SMBs. And that's for businesses that have disciplined reporting practices. Many skip reports entirely because it's too time-consuming.

The Automated Workflow

Report generation is one of the easiest processes to automate in small business:

  1. Scheduled trigger fires (e.g., every Monday at 8am for weekly sales reports, or the 1st of each month for financial dashboards)
  2. Data automatically pulled from your CRM, accounting software, marketing tools, inventory system — whatever sources you need
  3. Pre-built template populated with the latest data (Google Sheets, Excel, or specialised reporting tools)
  4. PDF generated automatically with professional formatting
  5. Email sent to stakeholder list with the report attached and a summary in the email body

The ROI

Time Saved: 4-6 hours per week

Common Reports to Automate:

  • Weekly sales performance by rep or region
  • Monthly financial dashboards with P&L and cash flow
  • Customer activity and retention metrics
  • Inventory levels and reorder alerts
  • Marketing campaign performance

Bonus Benefit: Once you automate basic reports, you can move to real-time dashboards that update continuously. Instead of looking at last week's numbers, you're making decisions based on today's data.

Many businesses implementing report automation discover they can finally create reports they always wanted but never had time for — like customer lifetime value tracking or product profitability analysis.

Process 4: Data Sync Between Systems

The Current Pain Point

You enter a new customer in your CRM with their contact details, billing address, and contract value. Then you open your accounting system and enter the same information again. A week later, the customer emails to update their address. You update it in the accounting system but forget to update the CRM. Now you have conflicting information, and nobody's sure which is correct.

This scenario plays out daily in SMBs running multiple business systems. For companies managing customer data across a CRM, accounting software, and perhaps an ecommerce platform or project management tool, data sync consumes 6-10 hours per week. Worse, the manual reconciliation creates version conflicts that damage customer relationships and business decisions.

The Automated Workflow

Proper data integration eliminates duplicate entry entirely:

  1. New customer created in CRM (your system of record for customer relationships)
  2. Customer automatically created in accounting system with all relevant details mapped correctly
  3. Customer updates in either system sync to the other — whether it's a new contact person, updated address, or changed payment terms
  4. Order placed in ecommerce platform triggers creation in both accounting (for invoicing) and CRM (for relationship tracking)
  5. Real-time or scheduled sync depending on your needs — mission-critical data syncs immediately, less urgent updates can batch hourly

The ROI

Time Saved: 6-10 hours per week

Error Reduction: 90%+ by establishing a single source of truth

Common Integration Examples:

  • HubSpot CRM ↔ QuickBooks Online
  • Salesforce ↔ Xero
  • Shopify ↔ Inventory management system
  • Project management tool ↔ Time tracking ↔ Accounting

The beauty of data sync automation is that benefits compound over time. The first week saves you 6-10 hours. The second week saves another 6-10 hours. A year later, you've recovered 300-500 hours and eliminated countless errors that would have damaged customer relationships.

Process 5: Email Follow-Ups

The Current Pain Point

You send a quote to a prospect and make a note to follow up in three days. Three days pass, life gets busy, and you forget. Or you remember but can't find the original email thread. Or you follow up but use inconsistent messaging. Meanwhile, your competitor who has disciplined follow-up processes closes the deal.

Manual follow-up tracking consumes 3-4 hours per week for most sales teams. But the real cost isn't time — it's the revenue you leave on the table from missed follow-ups. Studies show that businesses lose 30% or more of potential deals simply because they didn't follow up at the right time.

The Automated Workflow

Email follow-up automation ensures no opportunity slips through:

  1. Trigger event occurs — quote sent, demo completed, trial started, proposal delivered, first meeting held
  2. Automated follow-up sequence launches with timing proven to work:
    • Day 1: "Thanks for your time today — here's what we discussed"
    • Day 3: "Did you have a chance to review the proposal?"
    • Day 7: "Any questions I can answer to help your decision?"
    • Day 14: "Should we schedule a quick follow-up call?"
  3. Sequence stops automatically if the prospect replies or the deal status changes to won/lost
  4. Sales rep notified when prospect engages so they can step in for personal conversation

The ROI

Time Saved: 3-4 hours per week

Revenue Impact: 30%+ increase in quote-to-close rate for most businesses, simply by ensuring consistent follow-up

This workflow automation example works for more than just sales. Customer success teams use it for check-ins, marketing teams use it for lead nurturing, and HR teams use it for candidate follow-up. Any situation where you need to maintain contact over time benefits from automation.

The key is personalisation — these aren't generic email blasts. Each message references the specific interaction that triggered the sequence and includes relevant context from your CRM.

Practical Takeaways: How to Get Started

Now that you've seen five high-impact business processes to automate, let's talk about implementation. Here's your framework for deciding what to automate first and how to calculate the return.

How to Prioritise Which Process to Automate First

Use these four criteria to rank your opportunities:

  1. Time impact: Which process consumes the most hours each week? Quantify this — ask your team to track time for a week if you're not sure.
  2. Error risk: Which manual process creates costly errors? A single invoice processing mistake might cost R5,000 in late fees. Data entry errors in customer records damage relationships.
  3. Complexity: Start with simple automations before tackling complex multi-system workflows. Invoice processing and report generation are typically easier than customer onboarding.
  4. ROI calculation: Use the framework below to calculate payback period.

ROI Calculation Framework

Here's the formula to justify any automation investment:

Annual Value = Time saved per week x Hourly cost x 50 weeks

Payback Period = Implementation cost / Annual value

Example: Invoice processing saves 5 hours/week. Your bookkeeper costs R500/hour. That's R125,000 annual value (5 x 500 x 50). If implementation costs R30,000, your payback period is 2.4 months.

Target payback periods under 6 months for quick wins. Workflow automation should pay for itself fast.

Start Small, Scale Fast

The biggest mistake SMBs make with automation? Trying to automate everything at once. Instead:

  1. Pick ONE process from the five above
  2. Implement and test thoroughly before going live
  3. Measure actual results against your projections
  4. Expand to the next process once the first is running smoothly

This approach builds internal capability and confidence. Your team learns automation thinking, which makes subsequent implementations faster.

DIY vs. Hire Experts

Should you implement these automations yourself or hire specialists?

Choose DIY if:

  • The workflow is relatively simple (single trigger, few steps)
  • Someone on your team is comfortable with no-code tools like Make.com or Zapier
  • You have time to learn and can afford some trial and error
  • It's not a mission-critical process where mistakes are costly

Hire experts if:

  • The workflow involves complex integrations between multiple systems
  • It's a mission-critical process where reliability matters
  • You want it implemented correctly and quickly
  • You prefer to focus your team's time on core business activities

Many businesses start DIY for simple automations like email follow-ups, then bring in workflow automation specialists for complex integrations like data sync between systems.

Conclusion

Let's do the maths: automating these five processes saves 20-30 hours per week for most SMBs. That's 1,000-1,500 hours per year — roughly half a full-time employee. Except unlike hiring someone, automation works 24/7, never calls in sick, and doesn't make data entry errors.

More importantly, you're freeing up your team to focus on work that actually grows your business. Your IT manager stops manually generating reports and starts improving systems. Your operations team stops chasing invoices and starts optimising processes. Your sales team stops tracking follow-ups in spreadsheets and starts having more conversations with prospects.

The question isn't whether to automate these processes. It's how quickly you can get started.
