The problem

You Know Your Team Is Wasting Time. But Where?

If you're like most business leaders, you watch your team spend 20+ hours every week on tasks that could be automated. Manual data entry between systems. Waiting for approval chains that could be instant. Copy-pasting information from emails into spreadsheets. Re-keying customer details across multiple platforms.

These aren't just annoying inefficiencies. They're costing you real money in overtime, errors, and missed opportunities. When your marketing team manually uploads leads from spreadsheets, how many fall through the cracks? When your operations manager spends two hours every Monday compiling reports, what strategic work isn't getting done?

You know there's a better way. You've heard about workflow automation and business process automation, but the questions pile up: Where do you start? Which processes should you automate first? Is your business actually ready for this?

The uncertainty keeps you stuck while your competitors streamline their operations and pull ahead.

What's inside

Five Categories of Readiness Indicators

Data Management

How you currently handle data between systems. Are you manually transferring information? Are errors creeping in? This category reveals whether your data workflows are automation-ready.

Approval Workflows

Whether your approval processes create bottlenecks. Delayed sign-offs, chasing managers for responses, and status update meetings that could all be automated with smart routing.

Customer Communications

How you communicate with customers at every touchpoint. Manual follow-ups, onboarding emails, and status updates that should be triggered automatically based on customer actions.

Reporting

The time your team spends pulling data, formatting spreadsheets, and writing the same insights week after week. This category shows you where automated reporting saves the most hours.

Integration Readiness

Whether your existing tools can connect to each other. Modern platforms have APIs, but not all are equal. This assessment helps you understand what's possible with your current stack.
